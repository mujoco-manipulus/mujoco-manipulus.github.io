MuJoCo Manipulus is a robot learning benchmark for generalizable tool manipulation.

Abstract

We propose MuJoCo Manipulus, a novel open-source benchmark powered by the MuJoCo physics simulation engine, designed to accelerate advances in robot learning for manipulation.

Our benchmark includes a comprehensive set of standard manipulation tasks commonly used in the robotics community, with a particular emphasis on tool manipulation---a domain where the field currently lacks a unified benchmark. Different research groups rely on custom-designed tasks or closed-source setups, limiting cross-comparability and hindering significant progress in this field.

To that end, our benchmark provides a diverse set of challenging tool manipulation tasks, including variants of Pouring, Stacking, Scooping, Gathering, Hammering, Scraping, Ping Pong, and Mini-Golf.

The benchmark supports both state-based and vision-based observation spaces, and accommodates different modes of tool interaction, from free-floating tools that can be directly controlled, to tools that a robot arm learns to grasp and manipulate. Our benchmark is fully integrated with the Gymnasium API and seamlessly connects with widely used Deep Reinforcement Learning libraries, ensuring easy adoption by the community.
