Abstract

We propose MuJoCo Manipulus, a novel simulation benchmark powered by the MuJoCo physics simulation engine, designed to accelerate advances in learning for tool manipulation.

Our benchmark includes a comprehensive set of tool manipulation tasks commonly used in the robotics community---a domain where the field currently lacks a unified benchmark. Different research groups rely on custom-designed tool manipulation tasks and closed-source setups, limiting cross-comparability and hindering significant progress in this field.

To that end, our benchmark provides a diverse set of challenging tool manipulation tasks, including variants of Gathering, Mini-Golf, Hammering, Ping-Pong, Pouring, Scooping, Scraping, and Stacking.

The benchmark supports both state-based and vision-based observation spaces, and provides users with direct control of tools and robots which can grasp and manipulate tools. Our benchmark is fully integrated with the Gymnasium API and seamlessly connects with widely used Deep Reinforcement Learning libraries, ensuring easy adoption by the community.

MuJoCo Manipulus provides a general-purpose framework for building Deep Reinforcement Learning tasks on top of the MuJoCo Physics Engine. Success rate curves are shown for the 16 tasks in MuJoCo Manipulus with 3 Model-Free RL Baselines: CrossQ, SAC, and PPO.

Success Rates are reported every 50 episodes as the average success over the last 50 episodes, and each baseline result is averaged over 5 seeds with shading for the 95% CIs.
